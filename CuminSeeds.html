Introduction

Cumin seeds, the dried seeds of the Cuminum cyminum plant, are a fundamental spice in various global cuisines, known for their distinctive earthy, warm, and slightly bitter flavor. These small, elongated seeds are not only used to enhance the taste of dishes but also hold significant medicinal properties. India is the largest producer and exporter of cumin seeds, supplying a major portion of the global market. The seeds are cultivated in regions with arid and semi-arid climates, which are ideal for producing high-quality cumin.
